Ashlesh S Acharya

I'm someone who refuses to be boxed into one lane. By day I navigate the world of technology and business, learning the language of systems and solutions. By instinct, I'm drawn to older questions — about meaning, culture, and what it means to live well. I sing, I cycle, I act, I write — not because I've mastered any of it, but because I'm still curious. This is a profile of someone mid-journey, not someone who's arrived.

SIDE A — TRACK 01

On being a jack of all trades

"Jack of all trades, master of none" — I used to believe that too.

Until I noticed something. I could never fully commit to just one passion — the moment I picked one thing to specialize in, ten other interests started pulling at me, and I was never quite satisfied. But the moment I stopped trying to choose, and just moved between all of them — a day for cycling, a night for writing, a morning for voice practice, an evening for singing — that rotation became the thing that actually satisfied me.

Not mastery of one. Immersion in many, in parallel. So maybe I'll land a role as a Business Analyst. But I'll come home and still write, still sing, still train my voice, still create. Doing it all, side by side, isn't a compromise — it's what my soul actually asked for. Not a lane. A rhythm.

I'm also, underneath all of it, a fairly emotional person who can't help thinking about things philosophically — and at the same time, a practical one who likes getting things done. Both are true. Neither cancels the other out.
